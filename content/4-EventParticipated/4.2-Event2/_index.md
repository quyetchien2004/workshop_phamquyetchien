# Event 2: FCAJ Meetup - Career Sharing

## Event Information

* **Event name:** FCAJ Meetup - Career Sharing
* **Time:** June 13, 2026
* **Location:** AWS Office, Ho Chi Minh City
* **Role:** Attendee

## Main Content

In this event, I participated as an attendee. The main content focused on career sharing in the technology field, especially DevOps, AWS Community, Data Analytics Engineer, and how students can prepare themselves for working in large enterprise environments.

After the event, I reviewed the shared slides to summarize the key points and lessons learned.

### DevOps career direction

Trong Truong's session helped me understand more clearly what DevOps looks like in real work. Before this session, I often thought DevOps mainly meant CI/CD, Docker, Kubernetes, or cloud. However, the slides showed that DevOps is not only about tools. It is also a mindset for making systems stable and helping teams work more effectively.

Key points:

* Important fundamentals include Linux, networking, Git, CI/CD, containers, and understanding how applications run in real environments.
* Copying commands is not the same as understanding them.
* DevOps engineers should ask "why" before asking "how".
* Communication is part of the job because DevOps often works with many teams.
* A good DevOps engineer should automate repeatable work, think in systems, and use AI to enhance skills without turning off critical thinking.

### From First Cloud AI Journey to AWS Partner

Hoang Hieu Nghi's session shared the journey from First Cloud AI Journey to AWS Community Builder, AWS Student Builder Group, and AWS Partner. This helped me see that joining a community is not only about learning technical knowledge. It can also create opportunities for networking and career development.

Key points:

* First Cloud AI Journey is a good starting point for students to approach AWS and cloud.
* AWS Community Builder and AWS Student Builder Group provide more opportunities to share, connect, and build a personal profile.
* Getting the first job is only the beginning. Each person still needs to keep learning and write their own career story.
* Possible career directions include Solutions Architect, DevOps Engineer, Platform Engineer, and Software Engineer.

### Real stories and culture in multinational companies

Cuong Nguyen and Dat Pham's session discussed real work experience in multinational companies. The content covered recruitment processes, company culture, and career development mindset.

Key points:

* MNC recruitment processes often include CV screening, initial interviews, ability tests, technical interviews, and culture fit evaluation.
* Company culture is the way people think, work, and collaborate inside an organization.
* In technology environments, no-blame post-mortem culture is important because it helps teams focus on root causes instead of blaming individuals.
* Students should develop critical thinking, communication skills, problem-solving skills, and data storytelling.

### Data Analytics Engineer in practice

One part of the event also shared what a Data Analytics Engineer does in real work. I learned that this role is not only about creating dashboards or reports. It also requires understanding business problems, finding the root cause of metric changes, and proposing data-driven solutions.

Example tasks:

* Building daily, weekly, monthly, or quarterly reports to monitor operational performance.
* Designing dashboards to track data trends and detect anomalies.
* Analyzing business metrics, identifying root causes, and proposing improvements.
* Collaborating with different departments to solve real business problems.

## Lessons Learned

After joining Event 2, I learned several lessons:

* To follow DevOps or cloud, I need strong fundamentals instead of only learning tools at the surface level.
* Joining communities such as FCAJ gives students more opportunities to learn, connect, and clarify career direction.
* Communication, critical thinking, and problem presentation are as important as technical skills when preparing for work.
* In enterprise environments, learning attitude and collaboration strongly affect long-term growth.
* Whether I work as a DevOps Engineer, Software Engineer, or Data Analytics Engineer, I need to understand real problems and create value for the team.

## Personal Contribution

In this event, I participated as an attendee. I followed the sessions, reviewed the slides after the event, and summarized the content for my internship report. These sessions gave me a clearer view of career paths and the skills I should prepare after the internship program.
